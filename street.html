STREET FOOD DELIGHT OF GOA

Goa’s street food is a vibrant mix of spicy, savory, and flavorful delights, enjoyed by locals and tourists alike. Popular items include the famous Ros Omelette, a fluffy omelet served with spicy gravy and Goan bread (poi), and Cutlet Pao, crispy beef or chicken cutlets stuffed in soft bread. Choris Pao, made with Goan pork sausages, is a local favorite for its smoky, tangy taste. You’ll also find Mirchi Bhaji, deep-fried spicy green chilies, and Batata Vada, a spicy mashed potato fritter. These street eats are commonly found near beaches, markets, and roadside stalls, offering a true Goan experience.


Some Street Food include:
  • Ros Omelette – a fluffy omelet served with spicy gravy and Goan bread (poi)
  • Cutlet Pao – crispy beef or chicken cutlets stuffed in soft bread
  • Choris Pao – made with Goan pork sausages, known for its smoky, tangy taste
  • Mirchi Bhaji – deep-fried spicy green chilies
  • Batata Vada – a spicy mashed potato fritter
  • Perfect Pairings: Often enjoyed with hot chai or cold drinks near beach shacks and night stalls
